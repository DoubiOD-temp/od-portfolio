How it works: Pretext measures exactly where each character of "Ondrej Dobiš" belongs in a large heading. On load, those characters start as randomly scattered particles with Brownian motion. After a beat, each particle gets a force toward its target (x, y) position calculated by pretext. They accelerate, overshoot slightly, and settle with spring damping. Click to reverse — characters explode outward, drift, then re-summon.

How it works: Multiple paragraphs of bio text stacked vertically. Scroll position acts as a "formation threshold" — paragraphs near the top of the viewport are fully assembled into readable text. As you scroll down, each new paragraph transitions from scattered particles into formed text. Below the viewport = pure chaos. Scrolling back up dissolves text back into particles. It's like reading creates order from noise.

How it works: A draggable vertical line splits the canvas. Characters to the right of the line are snapped to their pretext-calculated positions (readable text). Characters to the left are in free-floating particle mode with physics. As you drag the line, characters crossing the boundary animate between states. Drag all the way right = full chaos. All the way left = fully readable. The transition zone at the boundary has a smooth interpolation — characters near the line are partially formed.
