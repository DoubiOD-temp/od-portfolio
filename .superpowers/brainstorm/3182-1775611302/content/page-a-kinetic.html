Page: Kinetic Typography (Experiment3)

3 variations stacked on one page. Each showcases a different text-physics interaction.

Variation 1 — "Magnetic Cursor"
Ondrej Dobiš
Move cursor near text → letters repel away from pointer
Move cursor away → letters spring back to original positions
Uses your name + tagline as content

How it works: Pretext calculates exact character positions for your name and tagline. Each character becomes a physics body with a spring force pulling it back to its "home" position. The cursor acts as a repulsion field — characters flee from it, then wobble back when you move away. Feels like touching a pool of water made of letters.

Click anywhere → all characters fall with gravity toward click point
They pile up, then slowly float back to their lines
Uses a prose paragraph as content

How it works: A full paragraph rendered to canvas via pretext. On click, every character gets a velocity toward the click point + gravity. Characters collide and pile up at the bottom. After 2 seconds, a "restore" force gradually pulls each character back to its original line position. Multiple clicks = cumulative chaos.

Variation 3 — "Wind Tunnel"
Developer, designer,
and problem solver based
in Bratislava, Slovakia.
I build things for the web.
Scroll or drag horizontally → "wind" blows characters off their lines
Characters stream rightward like leaves in a gale
Release → they spiral back into formation
Uses bio text as content

How it works: Bio text laid out by pretext. Horizontal drag/scroll applies a "wind" force — characters detach from their positions and stream horizontally with varying speeds (lighter characters like dots and commas fly faster). Characters leave trails. Release and they spiral back like a reversed explosion. The force is proportional to drag speed.
